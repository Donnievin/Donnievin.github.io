There is soooooo much linear algebra material available out there that it is actually hard to pick a single textbook to follow. 
While I was originally taught LA from the first reference below, I couldn't help but fall back in love with the topic after seeing the amazing 3B1B animations and understood it even deeper after the MIT series put together by [Dr. Gilbert Strang](https://web.mit.edu/18.06/www/). 
As a result, I have taken selections and problems from all of these to help put these notes together.

  - [LA by David C Lay, Steven R Lay, and Judi McDonald](https://broman.dev/download/Linear%20Algebra%20and%20its%20Applications%205th%20Edition.pdf)
  - [3 Blue 1 Brown](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLZHQObOWTQDPD3MizzM2xVFitgF8hE_ab)
  - [LA by Gilbert Strang](https://jcer.in/jcer-docs/E-Learning/Digital%20Library%20/E-Books/linear-algebra-author-gilbert-strang.pdf)
